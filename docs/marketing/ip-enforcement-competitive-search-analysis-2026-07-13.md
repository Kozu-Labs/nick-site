# IP Enforcement Competitive and Search Analysis

**Prepared:** July 2026
**Site:** The Law Office of Nicholas Lee
**Decision:** Expand the authority site around distinct user problems, not a generic practice-area catalog.

## Executive conclusion

The search landscape leaves a useful opening for Nicholas Lee: a plaintiff-side, attorney-first explanation of marketplace IP enforcement that connects legal analysis to organized factual investigation without presenting software as counsel.

Most ranking pages fall into one of four weaker patterns:

1. broad patent-enforcement claims supported mainly by verdicts, contingency terms, or superlatives;
2. Amazon seller-defense pages written for accused sellers rather than rights owners;
3. brand-protection pages that collapse platform reporting, monitoring, and litigation into one sales pitch; or
4. institutional thought leadership that is accurate but disconnected from a clear referral path.

Nick should occupy the space between a help article and a case-results page. The site should answer the practical questions a rights owner or referring lawyer asks before substance can begin: What right is implicated? What can the public marketplace record establish? Is the conduct isolated or repeated? Is a platform report enough? If multiple sellers are involved, what still must be established before considering one action?

### 2. Brand owner with a marketplace problem

Representative queries:

- Amazon trademark infringement attorney
- Amazon copyright infringement lawyer
- Amazon patent enforcement lawyer
- Amazon brand enforcement attorney
- online marketplace infringement lawyer

User job: distinguish ordinary platform reporting from a matter that may require factual investigation, legal analysis, or litigation.

### 3. Referring counsel looking for a procedural or operational answer

Representative queries:

- Schedule A litigation attorney
- Schedule A patent case lawyer
- sue multiple Amazon sellers in one case
- multi-seller intellectual property enforcement
- marketplace investigation for patent litigation

User job: find a lawyer who understands the procedural questions and can evaluate a referral without requiring a finished claim chart or a fully collected record.

### 4. Counsel or owner who does not know the category name

Representative questions:

- can I sue multiple online sellers together
- what information does a patent enforcement lawyer need
- when is Amazon Brand Registry not enough
- how do I document marketplace infringement
- what is a Schedule A case

User job: learn the category in plain language, including its limits, and reach the correct intake path.

## Content and writing findings

### What search engines are rewarding

- Exact intent alignment in titles and H1s.
- A direct answer near the top of the page.
- Dedicated pages for Amazon, trademark, copyright, and Schedule A questions.
- Practical intake detail: what the reader should gather, what the lawyer evaluates, and what happens next.
- Authority signals: named attorneys, verifiable credentials, primary-source links, clear authorship, and focused internal links.
- Enough topical depth to resolve the query without manufacturing length.

### What readers are likely to distrust

- "Aggressive," "cutting-edge," "comprehensive solutions," "proven results," and similar law-firm filler.
- Repeated three-item lists that say the same thing at different levels of abstraction.
- Generic statements that infringement is "complex" or that marketplaces are "fast-moving."
- Unqualified language implying that many defendants may always be joined or that a temporary restraining order is routine.
- Platform language that treats an Amazon complaint as equivalent to a legal conclusion.
- Pages that move from a statutory definition directly to a sales CTA without explaining the decision in between.

### House writing standard

Write like a working litigator explaining the next decision to another lawyer:

- Begin with the factual question, not a claim about the firm.
- Use short paragraphs and concrete nouns: claim limitation, listing, seller account, registration, product family, source page, accused use.
- State what a source can and cannot establish.
- Separate patent, trademark, and copyright analysis; do not call all suspected copying "counterfeiting."
- Describe Schedule A as a procedural form that depends on the facts, the asserted rights, joinder, jurisdiction, and current court requirements.
- End with the smallest non-confidential first step.

## Recommended information architecture

### Ship now

1. `/patent-enforcement`
   **Job:** Help a patent owner or referring lawyer understand the evaluation sequence from claim to marketplace surface to enforcement path.

2. `/trademark-infringement`
   **Job:** Separate suspected source confusion or counterfeit use from unauthorized resale, listing-policy issues, and other non-trademark disputes.

3. `/copyright-infringement`
   **Job:** Explain what to preserve when product photography, artwork, text, packaging, or other original content appears across listings, and why registration timing matters.

4. `/amazon-ip-enforcement`
   **Job:** Explain the choice among platform tools, factual investigation, and litigation across patent, trademark, and copyright problems.

5. `/insights` with three substantive pages:
   - `/insights/schedule-a-is-not-automatic`
   - `/insights/what-to-send-an-ip-enforcement-lawyer`
   - `/insights/marketplace-investigation-before-filing`

These pages are sufficiently distinct to avoid doorway-page duplication. They should cross-link by decision, not repeat the same general introduction.

### Wait

- Separate Amazon patent, Amazon trademark, and Amazon copyright landing pages. The search intent can be served honestly by one strong Amazon page plus the right-specific pages.
- Representative matters, case results, or damages content until public-record corroboration and attorney-advertising review are complete.
- Fee and contingency pages until the firm's actual engagement models are verified and approved.
- Platform-by-platform pages for Walmart, eBay, Etsy, Temu, or AliExpress until there is original material substantial enough to make each page useful.

## Page theses

### Patent enforcement

The claim sets the search. A marketplace investigation is useful only when the product features being collected map to a defined patent question and counsel can distinguish a promising lead from a filing record.

### Trademark infringement

A copied name or logo is not the whole analysis. The inquiry begins with ownership and priority, then asks how the mark is used, what goods are involved, and whether the marketplace presentation could confuse purchasers about source or sponsorship.

### Copyright infringement

The useful first record is the work, the ownership/registration history, and the accused material side by side. A platform complaint may remove content, but it does not answer every authorship, license, copying, or litigation question.

### Amazon IP enforcement

Amazon offers reporting tools for suspected patent, trademark, and copyright violations. Counsel adds value by choosing the right theory, preserving the right facts, assessing conduct beyond one ASIN, and deciding whether the problem ends with platform action or requires a broader response.

### Schedule A explainer

"Schedule A" describes how defendants are identified in certain multi-defendant complaints; it is not a shortcut around joinder, jurisdiction, service, or proof. The fit is matter-specific and current judicial requirements matter.

### Referral checklist

The best initial referral is not a document dump. Public right identifiers, one representative product or use, the suspected pattern, and the desired role are enough to clear conflicts and define the first research question.

### Marketplace investigation

A listing can establish what was publicly represented at a point in time. It may not establish product behavior, seller identity, common control, sales, jurisdiction, or infringement without additional work.

## Source and citation standard

Each substantive page should include:

- a direct answer in the opening section;
- a visible `Updated July 2026` line;
- primary-source links near the proposition they support;
- a short sources section rather than academic footnotes;
- `Article` or `LegalService` schema only where the visible page supports it;
- no FAQ schema unless the same questions and answers are visible;
- internal links to the relevant practice page, attorney-referral page, Schedule A explainer, and conflicts-safe contact path.

## Falsifying tests

The expansion does not ship if any of these is true:

1. A reader believes every marketplace problem is suitable for a Schedule A case.
2. A cold reader cannot tell whether a page is written for a rights owner or an accused seller.
3. The Amazon page merely restates Brand Registry help without explaining the legal decision layer.
4. Any page implies a result, fee arrangement, credential, or attorney review that is not verified.
5. Three consecutive paragraphs could be moved to another IP law firm's site without changing a concrete noun.
